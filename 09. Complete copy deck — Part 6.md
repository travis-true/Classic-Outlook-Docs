## Page 51 — Before using Copilot  
## Before using Copilot  
Copilot can help you work through email faster, but you remain responsible for every message you send and every action you take.  
## Copilot can help you  
* Summarize an email conversation  
* Identify key points in a thread  
* Create a first draft  
* Adjust tone or length  
* Improve clarity  
* Review reader sentiment  
* Suggest ways to strengthen a message  
## Copilot does not replace  
* Business judgment  
* Customer knowledge  
* Policy requirements  
* Required approvals  
* Accurate records  
* Human review  
* Your responsibility for the final message  
## Requirements  
Copilot in Classic Outlook requires:  
* A company Microsoft 365 work account  
* An assigned Microsoft 365 Copilot license  
* A supported version of Microsoft 365 Apps  
* A primary mailbox hosted in Exchange Online  
* Connected experiences and required organizational settings  
Copilot in Outlook works with Classic Outlook and New Outlook, but mailbox-based features are supported only for the user’s primary Exchange Online mailbox.  
## Copilot is not available for  
* Shared mailboxes  
* Delegate mailboxes  
* Group mailboxes  
* Archive mailboxes  
* Unsupported or on-premises mailboxes  
* Some protected, signed or encrypted messages  
## Safe-use reminders  
Before using Copilot:  
1. Confirm that you are working in your primary work mailbox.  
2. Use appropriate business content.  
3. Ask for a specific result.  
4. Review the original source.  
5. Check names, dates, owners and deadlines.  
6. Remove unnecessary sensitive details.  
7. Follow existing data-handling requirements.  
8. Complete the final review yourself.  
## Use low-risk practice content  
When learning a Copilot feature, begin with a message that:  
* Does not contain sensitive customer information  
* Does not require an immediate response  
* Has details you can easily verify  
* Will not create risk if the first draft needs significant editing  
## What to remember  
Copilot can produce confident wording that is incomplete or incorrect. Verify the output against the original email.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not use Copilot in a shared mailbox and assume it will use the shared mailbox content.  
* Do not send a Copilot draft without reading it.  
* Do not treat inferred details as confirmed facts.  
* Do not include more sensitive information than the task requires.  
* Do not use Copilot output as a replacement for required approvals.

## Page 52 — Summarize an email conversation  
## Summarize an email conversation with Copilot  
## Use this when  
Catch up on a long conversation before deciding what requires attention.  
## Before you start  
Confirm that:  
* The conversation is in your primary mailbox.  
* The message is supported by Copilot.  
* The thread contains enough context to summarize.  
* You can verify the summary against the original messages.  
## Create the summary  
1. Open or select the email conversation.  
2. Look at the top of the email thread.  
3. Select **Summary by Copilot** or **Summarize**.  
4. Wait while Copilot reviews the conversation.  
5. Read the summary displayed above the email.  
6. Review any numbered citations included in the summary.  
The command wording may vary between **Summary by Copilot** and **Summarize**.  
## Use summary citations  
When numbered citations appear:  
1. Select a citation.  
2. Review the corresponding message in the conversation.  
3. Confirm that the summarized point matches the source.  
4. Return to the summary.  
5. Continue verifying important details.

## Page 53 — Review a Copilot summary  
## Review the summary before acting  
A useful summary may still omit context, combine details incorrectly or describe an implied action as though it were confirmed.  
## Check the summary  
Review:  
* Main topic  
* Decisions  
* Questions  
* Requested actions  
* Owners  
* Deadlines  
* Customer or producer details  
* Open issues  
* Attachments or linked information  
* Latest message in the conversation  
## Verification process  
1. Read the Copilot summary.  
2. Identify each important statement.  
3. Open the related source message.  
4. Confirm the wording and context.  
5. Note anything missing from the summary.  
6. Identify the actual next action.  
7. Make that follow-up visible in Outlook.  
## Summary review table  

| Check         | Question                                     |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Topic         | Does the summary describe the correct issue? |
| Decision      | Was a decision actually made?                |
| Action        | Is the requested task stated accurately?     |
| Owner         | Was the person explicitly assigned?          |
| Deadline      | Was the date stated in the thread?           |
| Context       | Is an important condition missing?           |
| Latest update | Did the summary include the newest message?  |
  
****Example****  
**Copilot summary says:** The team agreed to complete the update by Friday.  
**Original thread says:** Jordan suggested Friday, but the date was not confirmed.  
**Correct interpretation:** The deadline is not yet confirmed.  
**Next action:** Ask the owner to confirm the deadline.  
## Classic Outlook limitation  
Classic Outlook can summarize the conversation, but it does not provide the same conversational Copilot Chat experience available in New Outlook and Outlook on the web.  
You may not be able to ask follow-up questions such as:  
* What deadlines are missing?  
* Who owns each action?  
* What changed between the first and last message?  
Use the summary, citations and original thread to complete that review manually.  
## Attachment limitation  
Attachment summarization for supported PDF, Word and PowerPoint files is available in New Outlook and Outlook on the web. Do not assume the same attachment-summary option is available in Classic Outlook.  
## What to remember  
A summary shortens the reading. It does not remove the need to verify.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not act only from the summary.  
* Do not assume an implied date is a confirmed deadline.  
* Do not assume the summary includes every attachment.  
* Do not treat a suggested action as an assigned action.  
* Do not overlook the newest message in the thread.

## Page 54 — Identify actions, owners and deadlines  
## Identify actions, owners and deadlines  
## Use this when  
Turn the conversation summary into a clear follow-up plan.  
Because Classic Outlook does not include the full conversational Copilot Chat experience, complete this review using:  
* The Copilot summary  
* Summary citations  
* The original messages  
* Your business knowledge  
## Review the conversation  
1. Generate the Copilot summary.  
2. Read the original thread.  
3. Identify every requested action.  
4. Determine whether an owner is explicitly named.  
5. Identify any stated date or deadline.  
6. Mark missing details as **Not specified**.  
7. Decide which actions belong to you.  
8. Choose how each action will remain visible.  
## Action review table  

| Action | Owner | Deadline | Source | Status |
| ----------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------------- | -------------- | ----------------------- |
| [Describe action] | [Name or Not specified] | [Date or Not specified] | [Message/date] | [Open/Waiting/Complete] |
| [Describe action] | [Name or Not specified] | [Date or Not specified] | [Message/date] | [Open/Waiting/Complete] |
  
****Use exact language****  
When the thread does not name an owner, write:  
Not specified  
When the thread does not give a deadline, write:  
Not specified  
When the message suggests but does not confirm a date, write:  
Proposed, not confirmed  
## Example  

| Action | Owner | Deadline | Status |
| ---------------------- | ------------- | ---------------- | ------------------- |
| Confirm effective date | Jordan | Not specified | Open |
| Update renewal summary | Taylor | June 18 | Open |
| Send final response | Not specified | Proposed June 20 | Needs clarification |
  
****What to remember****  
Missing information is a follow-up need. Do not fill gaps with assumptions.

## Page 55 — Make Copilot-assisted follow-up visible  
## Turn the review into action  
After identifying the next steps, use Outlook to make them visible.  
## Choose the follow-up tool  

| Need                        | Outlook action                              |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Return to the email later   | Add a flag                                  |
| Receive an alert            | Add a reminder                              |
| Show the work status        | Add a category                              |
| Reserve focused work time   | Create a calendar cue                       |
| Ask for missing information | Draft a reply                               |
| Wait for another person     | Categorize as Waiting and set a review date |
| Work is complete            | Mark complete and file the message          |
  
****Process****  
1. Confirm the action.  
2. Confirm the owner.  
3. Confirm the deadline.  
4. Ask for any missing details.  
5. Choose the correct follow-up method.  
6. Record the next action.  
7. Complete the work.  
8. Close the loop.  
## Example  
**Email thread:** Producer asks for an updated proposal, but the effective date is unclear.  
**Copilot summary:** The producer requested an updated proposal and referenced two possible effective dates.  
**Verified next action:** Ask which effective date should be used.  
**Outlook follow-up:**  
* Draft the clarification reply  
* Flag the message for tomorrow  
* Categorize as Waiting after sending  
* Set a Friday review reminder  
## What to remember  
Copilot can help you understand the conversation. Outlook tools help you follow through.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not stop after generating the summary.  
* Do not leave identified actions only in notes.  
* Do not mark a message complete while required information is missing.  
* Do not assign work to another person unless ownership is confirmed.

## Page 56 — Draft an email with Copilot  
## Draft an email with Copilot

3. In the message window, locate the **Copilot** icon on the toolbar.  
4. Select the Copilot icon.  
5. Select **Draft with Copilot**.

8. Wait for Copilot to create the draft.

1. Open the email.  
2. Select **Reply** or **Reply All**.  
3. Select the Copilot icon in the message toolbar.  
4. Select **Draft with Copilot**.  
5. Enter instructions describing the response you need.  
6. Select **Generate**.  
Copilot may use the email conversation as context when drafting the reply.

## Page 57 — Build a clear prompt  
## Build a clear prompt  
A clear prompt has four parts.  

| Part        | Question                                 |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Goal        | What do you want Copilot to do?          |
| Context     | What background matters?                 |
| Source      | What email or information should it use? |
| Expectation | What tone, length or format do you need? |
  
****Prompt formula****  
**[Goal]** using **[source and context]**. Make it **[tone, length and format]**. Include **[required information]**. Do not **[important restriction]**.  
## Example: Ask for missing information  
Draft a warm professional reply to this thread. Confirm that I received the request, ask for the missing effective date and keep the reply under 100 words. Do not add facts that are not in the thread.  
## Example: Confirm next steps  
Draft a reply confirming the next steps, owners and deadlines stated in this thread. Use bullets. If a detail is not stated, leave it out.  
## Example: Provide a status update  
Draft a concise status update using the information in this thread. Include completed work, open items and the next expected update. Keep it under 150 words.  
## Example: Clarify a request  
Draft a friendly reply asking the sender to confirm the requested coverage period and deadline. Keep the questions easy to answer.  
## Add useful constraints  
Tell Copilot to:  
* Keep the same facts  
* Do not add new information  
* Use bullets  
* Keep it under a word limit  
* Ask one clear question  
* Put the requested action first  
* Use a warm professional tone  
* Mark missing details as not specified  
## What to remember  
Better instructions improve the draft, but they do not guarantee accuracy.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not use a vague prompt such as “Reply to this.”  
* Do not ask Copilot to invent missing details.  
* Do not include unnecessary sensitive information in the prompt.  
* Do not assume Copilot knows business context that is not in the message.

## Page 58 — Review and adjust the Copilot draft  
## Review the generated draft  
After Copilot generates the message:  
1. Read the entire draft.  
2. Compare it with your prompt.  
3. Compare it with the original email.  
4. Check names, facts, dates and commitments.  
5. Decide whether to keep, adjust or regenerate it.  
## Adjust the result  
When the draft is close but needs improvement:  
1. Select **Adjust**.  
2. Choose an available tone or length option.  
3. Review the revised result.  
Available choices may include options such as:  
* Direct  
* Neutral  
* Casual  
* Formal  
* Short  
* Medium  
* Long  
Available labels may vary by version.  
## Edit the prompt  
1. Select the prompt text.  
2. Add or change the instructions.  
3. Select **Generate** again.  
4. Compare the new draft with the previous result.  
## Regenerate the draft  
1. Select **Regenerate**.  
2. Review the new version.  
3. Decide which version is more useful.  
## Keep the draft  
When the result is ready for manual editing:  
1. Select **Keep**.  
2. Edit the message directly.  
3. Add information only you know.  
4. Remove unnecessary content.  
5. Complete the final review.  
6. Select **Send**.

## Page 59 — Improve tone, clarity or length  
## Improve a draft with Copilot  
## Use this when  
The message is accurate but needs to be clearer, shorter, warmer or easier to act on.  
## Adjust a Copilot-generated draft  
1. Generate the draft.  
2. Select **Adjust**.  
3. Choose the desired tone or length.  
4. Review the revised message.  
5. Keep the version that best supports the reader.  
6. Edit it manually as needed.  
## Modify selected text  
When the feature is available:  
1. Select the text you want to improve.  
2. Select the Copilot icon.  
3. Choose an available rewriting option.  
4. Review the revised text.  
5. Confirm that the meaning did not change.  
## Useful instructions  
* Make this clearer.  
* Make this warmer.  
* Make this more concise.  
* Keep the same facts.  
* Put the requested action first.  
* Use bullets.  
* Keep this under 100 words.  
* Remove repeated information.  
* Make the questions easy to answer.  
* Use a confident but respectful tone.  
## Before and after example  
## Before  
I am following up because there are several different pieces of information that may need to be reviewed before we can continue with what was discussed previously.  
## Clearer  
Before we continue, please confirm the effective date and coverage period.  
## Review the change  
Check that Copilot did not:  
* Add a fact  
* Remove an important condition  
* Change the requested action  
* Change a deadline  
* Overstate certainty  
* Make the tone too casual or too formal  
* Remove required context  
## What to remember  
Improve the message without changing its meaning.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not ask for “more professional” without defining what needs improvement.  
* Do not accept a shorter draft that removes critical details.  
* Do not let a warmer tone weaken a clear deadline.  
* Do not use a formal tone that makes the request harder to understand.

## Page 61 — Create a meeting from an email  
## Create a meeting from an email  
## Use this when  
The email conversation requires discussion, coordination or a decision involving other people.  
Classic Outlook does not currently provide the same **Schedule with Copilot** workflow available in New Outlook. Create the meeting manually from the email.  
## Create a meeting from the message  
Depending on the Classic Outlook layout:  
1. Open or select the email.  
2. On the ribbon, select **Meeting** or **Reply with Meeting**.  
If that command is not visible:  
1. Drag the email to the **Calendar** icon.  
2. Open the resulting appointment.  
3. Select **Invite Attendees**.  
Then:  
1. Review the attendee list.  
2. Add or remove attendees.  
3. Replace the email subject with a clear meeting title.  
4. Choose the date and time.  
5. Add a short agenda.  
6. Remove unnecessary email content.  
7. Add an approved meeting location or online meeting link.  
8. Review the invitation.  
9. Select **Send**.  
## Suggested agenda structure  
**Purpose:** [What the group needs to accomplish]  
**Discuss:**  
* [Topic]  
* [Topic]  
* [Topic]  
**Decision or outcome needed:** [Required result]

## Page 62 — Review before sending  
## Review Copilot output before sending  
Before you send or use Copilot-generated content, check every item below.  
## Accuracy  
* Are all facts correct?  
* Did Copilot invent or assume anything?  
* Does the draft match the original email?  
* Are product, account or customer details accurate?  
## Recipients  
* Is the To field correct?  
* Does everyone in Cc need the message?  
* Is Reply All necessary?  
* Is the From address correct?  
## Actions and commitments  
* Is the requested action clear?  
* Is the owner correct?  
* Is the deadline confirmed?  
* Did the draft create an unauthorized commitment?  
* Are missing details clearly identified?  
## Tone  
* Would you say this to the recipient?  
* Is the wording respectful and direct?  
* Is the message too formal or too casual?  
* Is the urgency appropriate?  
## Completeness  
* Did you add information only you know?  
* Is necessary context missing?  
* Are attachments or links included?  
* Are permissions correct?  
* Is the subject still accurate?  
## Sensitive details  
* Does the message include only necessary information?  
* Is the recipient authorized to receive it?  
* Did Copilot repeat sensitive details unnecessarily?  
* Are forwarded or quoted messages appropriate to include?  
## Final wording  
* Does this sound like you?  
* Is it easy to understand?  
* Is the requested action easy to find?  
* Is the message concise enough?  
* Did you complete a final proofreading?
